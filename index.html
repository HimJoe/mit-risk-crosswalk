Overview

As artificial intelligence continues to transform industries and societies, managing AI-related risks has become a critical priority for organizations worldwide. The MIT Risk Repository represents a comprehensive collection of AI risks across multiple domains, including general AI risks, technical vulnerabilities, governance challenges, operational considerations, and compliance requirements.

This interactive portal allows you to explore how various AI governance frameworks map to the MIT Risk Repository, examining their complementary strengths, identifying potential gaps, and providing recommendations for organizations looking to establish robust AI risk management practices.

Recommendations

Based on our analysis of how these frameworks map to the MIT Risk Repository, we recommend organizations:

Multi-Framework Approach

No single framework comprehensively addresses all MIT risk categories. Organizations should adopt complementary frameworks based on their specific risk profile.

Risk-Based Prioritization

Select frameworks that best address your highest-priority risks: NIST AI RMF and Singapore AI Verify for bias, IEEE standards for vulnerabilities, ISO/IEC 42001 for governance, EU AI Act for compliance.

Custom Controls for Gaps

Create tailored governance approaches for emerging risks with limited framework coverage, such as prompt injection vulnerabilities, model hallucination, and advanced adversarial attacks.

Cross-Functional Governance

Ensure technical, ethical, legal, and business perspectives are integrated into AI governance structures.

Continuous Monitoring

Develop metrics and monitoring capabilities that span multiple frameworks to ensure comprehensive risk coverage.

Regular Reassessment

As frameworks evolve and new risks emerge, organizations should periodically reassess their governance approaches to ensure continued effectiveness.

Pro Tip: Begin with a gap analysis using this crosswalk to identify your organization's most critical AI risk areas, then implement frameworks strategically to address those gaps.
